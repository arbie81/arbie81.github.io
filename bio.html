BIO

Alina Richard Brennaman (b. 1981, Kansas, USA) is a self-taught researcher, archivist, artist and writer. Her art centers on sound, radio, and experimental media, driven by a curiosity to understand, preserve and amplify voices that go against the statues quo and often remain unheard. With no formal academic background, she’s shaped her practice through years of immersion in underground music scenes, and independent world travel, always grounded in self-sufficiency and a hands-on approach. ARB has lived and traveled widely, drawing inspiration from places and cultures that often exist outside the mainstream. Her work evolves independently, documenting and experimenting with the sounds and histories of those who are frequently overlooked, while remaining untethered from conventional academic frameworks.
